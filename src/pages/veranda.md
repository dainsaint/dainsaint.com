<h2 class="text-display" style="font-weight: light">A five-week program to remember who you are, why you started, and where you're going next.</h2>

I had been running my own business for over a decade. But after ten years as a creative entrepreneur, I was burnt out, struggling, and completely lacking in motivation. I had spent so long going through the motions of running my business that it felt like the business was running me. I felt like I had completely forgotten who I was.

So I left.

Mid-project, I took off and went back to stay with my family in Jamaica. I hadn't been back there in over ten years — since before we'd started the company. And I needed something to remind me who I was.

And there, sitting on my grandfather's veranda, I realized the only thing that really mattered was that I was able to get back home, to sit on that veranda with friends and family, laugh over a beer, and just be.

**I'd like to help you find your personal veranda.**

## who this is for {.text-display}

You're a polymath. A creative business owner. Someone who does fifty things well and has spent years explaining yourself to people who only understand one.

**You're probably not burnt out on the *work***. You're burnt out feeling like there's no rhyme or reason to what you do. You know what you're drawn towards, what lights you up, and how it helps people — but you have no idea how to tell the _Story Of You_ to anyone, least of all yourself. And you're burnt out because you have no systems in place to support and reinforce who you're truly meant to be.

**Veranda is for people who need to fully embrace what they're actually doing, and why anyone should care.**

## the process {.text-display}

**Veranda** is business therapy disguised as positioning work.

We move through **five weekly 2-hour sessions**. We'll meet in person or via video, and move through a scaffold designed to get you from *"I don't know how to explain what I do"* to *"I can't believe I was making it this complicated."*

You come in with a fragmented sense of self. You walk away with a clear mission statement, a redesigned creative support system, and a concrete first step off the Veranda.
